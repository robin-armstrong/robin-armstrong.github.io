Research

I currently do research in randomized numerical linear algebra under the supervision of professor Anil Damle. We are interested in understanding how the accuracy of low-rank approximation algorithms is affected by problem-specific structures in the input matrix. Particularly, we are interested in the effects of leverage scores in the matrix's singular subspaces, as well as in developing rapidly computable a-posteriori error bounds for randomized interpolative decompositions.

My undergraduate thesis investigated novel uncertainty quantification inequalities based on variational representations of alpha-divergence. I completed this thesis under the supervision of professors Luc Rey-Bellet, Markos A. Katsoulakis, and Jeremiah Birrell at UMass Amherst.

During the summer of 2020, I participated in an REU at Clemson University under the supervision of professor Shuhong Gao. We investigated the security of zero-knowledge proofs based on Reed-Solomon codes and polynomial low-degree testing.

During the summer of 2019, I participated in an REU at UMass Amherst under the supervision of professor Yao Li. We constructed an event driven billiard simulation of a gas in thermal nonequilibrium to investigate heat flow and Fourier's law.
